ABOUT ME

I really enjoy traveling abroad. The opportunity to experience other cultures and ways of life has always excited me. For as far back as I can remember I always looked forward to our annual family travels and vacations.

Growing up I was involved in many sports. I took a strong interest in karate which I began around the age of 10. After competing in tournaments and completing the required challenges I received my purple belt -- two belts shy from Black -- all before my senior year of high school. In addition, fencing was another big interest of mine. In high school and undergrad I joined the fencing team and during middle school I actually played on a all boys basketball team and was pretty darn good at it. Other sports that I participated in were soccer and gymnastics.

Originally I was born in Washington D.C. and lived there until the age of three. From there my family and I lived in Plano, Texas, Ann Arbor, Michigan and the Chicagoland area. Now we call Chicago Home. I graduated from Homewood Flossmoor High School and obtained my first Bachelors degree from Knox College in Biochemistry and Modern Languages -- with a primary emphasis on Spanish and secondary emphasis on Mandarin. From there I went on to persue my second Bachelors degree in Vascular Ultrasound Technology from Rush University and graduated Summa Cum Laude. Currently, I work for Advocate Healthcare as their Lead Vascular Ultrasound Technologist.
